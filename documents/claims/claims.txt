1. Claim Process Docs  
To ensure efficient processing and minimize delays, the insured must submit a complete, signed claim 
file. The required documentation varies depending on the nature of the claim.  
1.1 Checklist for Standard Reimbursement Claims  
All documents must be submitted as original physical copies or clear, high -resolution digital scans:  
 Completed Claim Form:  Form A (filled and signed by the policyholder) and Form B (filled and 
signed by the treating medical practitioner).  
 Identity Proof:  A clear copy of the patient ’s government -issued ID (Passport, Driver's License, 
or National ID Card) and the original digital health insurance card.  
 Discharge Summary:  The official summary issued by the hospital, explicitly detailing the date of 
admission, date of discharge, presenting complaints, clinical findings, and treatment given.  
 Final Hospital Bill & Receipts:  Itemized final bill along with the corresponding payment receipts 
stamped by the hospital cash counter.  
 Diagnostic Reports:  All pharmacy bills accompanied by the doctor's prescriptions, and original 
reports for lab tests, X -rays, or MRI scans conducted during the treatment period.

2. Reimbursement Process  
When utilizing a non -network hospital, or if a network hospital's pre -authorization is denied for non -
medical reasons, the policyholder must pay the bills out -of-pocket and seek reimbursement.  
[Hospital Discharge] ──> [Gather All Original Docs] ──> [Submit via Portal/Post] ──> [Verification & 
Approval] ──> [Direct Bank Transf er] 
Step 1: Intimation of Claim  
The insurance company must be notified regarding the medical event within strict timelines to avoid 
claim rejection:  
 Planned Hospitalization:  Notify the company at least 48 hours  prior to admission.  
 Emergency Hospitalization:  Notify the company within 24 hours  of actual admission.  
 Note: Intimation can be done via the customer mobile app, email portal, or by calling our 24/7 
claims helpline.  
Step 2: Document Compilation and Upload  
1. Collect all original documents listed under Section 1.1.  
2. Log into the secure insurance customer portal.  
3. Navigate to Claims > File New Reimbursement . 
4. Scan and upload the documents chronologically : 
Admission documents ──> Lab Reports ──>Bills ──> Discharge Summary).  
Step 3: Assessment and  Verification  
Upon receipt, the Claims Assessment Team will review the file.  
 If any information is missing, a Deficiency Letter  will be issued via email. The customer has 14 
business days  to respond and provide the requested information.  
 A medical auditor may contact the treating hospital directly to verify medical records or billing 
details.  
Step 4: Settlement and Payout  
Once the claim is formally approved, the net payable amount (calculated after deducting non -medical 
expenses like registration fees or service charges) will be transferred directly via electronic bank 
transfer.  
 Processing TAT (Turnaround Time):  Payout occurs within 7 to 10 business days  from the date 
the final document or clarification is successfully accepted.
